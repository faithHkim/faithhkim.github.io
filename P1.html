FLIGHT DISPLAY

An opportunity to dive into the mechanics of flight, and to reimagine the flight display that pilots rely on while flying.

PROMPT

Based off of the Air France 447 accident, this project revolved around the obvious need for improvement in the flight deck design. There is more than one way of showing how a plane is flying and faring in external conditions. On top of that, its important for a pilot to know how the plane is flying in relationship to its flight envelope, which can indicate future stalling or further negative conditions. I sought to create a display that provided clear visual cues in safe and unsafe conditions.

PROCESS

We started by learning the mechanics a plane and how it flies. We heavily investigated and picked apart the Air France accident, where the nose of a commercial plane was mistakenly pointed to far up. This caused the plane to stall, and eventually leading to many deaths. While there was a definite lack of communication between the pilots, the flight display could have improved the situation with better visual communication. We then studied the Three Mile Island Accident, that had a similar issue of operators failing to recognize the situation and act accordingly. The class did a lot of research and analysis before even starting to design a new flight deck. I soon found that understanding the fundamentals of a simple interaction was an important factor, as I started to design.

FINAL SOLUTION

my final solution involved a horizontal view of a plane and a ghost plane counterpart. During a groupthink session, my group members and I were inspired by video games, like Dance Dance Revolution and Mario kart. These games had components where the player either had a bird’s eye view or motions that were coming next. Taking that into the context of a plane, I decided to bring in a ghost plane to illustrate the future path of the plane and the necessary steps to get there. The ghost plane functions with speed, altitude and angle optimizations that essentially tell the pilot how much to increase or decrease each in order to be on the ghost plane’s path.
